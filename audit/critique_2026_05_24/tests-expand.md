# B1 ŌĆö Test-coverage expansion audit (2026-05-24)

## Summary

Verified the starter "uncovered" list against actual test inventory: of 22 starter candidates, 9 turn out to already have at least one dedicated test file (most stub-level), 4 have only meta-snapshot or indirect coverage, and the remainder are partially exercised through suite-level integration but lack a focused public-API unit file. Concrete gaps with the biggest blast radius: `crash_reporting` (zero tests; Windows-signal path), `metrics_registry` (zero direct tests of the registry mutation API), `composite_auto_detect.detect_group_column_candidates` (no biz_value test despite being the gate for `linear_residual_grouped` and the proposed FHC reuse), `feature_selection.mi` legacy estimators (no parity tests across three implementations), `feature_selection.importance` (no behavioural unit tests; only `_meta` baselines), `feature_selection.optbinning` (no direct tests). Weak-assert clusters: PySR equation existence checks, ranker score-shape-only asserts, and the bruteforce biz_value file. Fuzz blind spots: no `crash_reporting` toggle axis, no `prefer_polarsds=True` ├Ś LTR combo, and no `recurrent_model_cfg` ├Ś `weight_schemas=recency-only` combo. Numba blind spots: every backend kernel marked below requires a `NUMBA_DISABLE_JIT=1` nightly to pick up real-line coverage.
