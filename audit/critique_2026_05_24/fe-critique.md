# A2 - Feature-engineering critique (read-only audit, 2026-05-24)

Scope: `feature_engineering/basic.py`, `feature_engineering/bruteforce.py`, `feature_engineering/timeseries.py`, `feature_engineering/transformer/random_features.py`, `feature_engineering/pysr_operators.py`, `training/composite_transforms.py`, `training/_composite_target_estimator.py`, `training/core/_phase_composite_discovery.py`, `training/core/_phase_composite_post.py`, `training/core/_phase_composite_wrapping.py`, `training/_pipeline_extensions.py`, `training/core/_phase_helpers_fit_pipeline.py` (date-feature wiring). Behavioural - no edits.

## Summary

The composite-target Transform registry is well-structured (frozen dataclass, fit/forward/inverse/domain contract, predict-side `y=None` sentinel, MAD soft-cap, K-cond gate, James-Stein shrinkage). `CompositeTargetEstimator` is sklearn-compatible with proper `clone` + `from_fitted_inner` paths and avoids polars->pandas materialisation. Genuine concerns clustered in three buckets: (a) cache + bool-cast paths in `apply_preprocessing_extensions` do `df.copy()` on the full pandas frame in violation of the 100GB rule; (b) `_apply_pysr_fe` and `run_pysr_feature_engineering` mutate the caller-passed frame by injecting a temp target column and renaming columns in place (renames leak back even when not requested); (c) `_phase_composite_discovery.copy(deep=False)` then per-column writes leak the freshly-injected target column onto sibling frames sharing the BlockManager. Date-feature wiring is solid; `compute_positional_encoding` is deterministic; RFF is seeded. Test coverage of `composite_transforms.py` Transform classes is largely funnelled through `CompositeTargetEstimator` integration rather than per-transform unit suites, leaving the extended (median_residual / quantile_residual / monotonic_residual / ewma_residual / rolling_quantile_ratio / frac_diff / chain_*) transforms thinly covered at the public-contract level.
