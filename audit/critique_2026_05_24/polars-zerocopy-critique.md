# A6 - polars <-> pandas <-> numpy zero-copy audit (2026-05-24)

## Summary

Scanned target dirs for every conversion site. Most polars->pandas calls are already routed through the project's Arrow split-blocks bridge (`get_pandas_view_of_polars_df`) - so the largest, hottest dataframe conversions are not the worst offenders. The real avoidable cost lives in two clusters: (1) `pd.DataFrame(<polars>)`-style constructors that bypass Arrow and silently densify (e.g. `_main_train_suite.py:814-816`, `_predict_pre_pipeline.py:149`, `_pipeline_helpers.py:401-402`, `_pipeline_extensions.py:497`, `target_temporal_audit.py:130`), and (2) per-column `.to_numpy()` loops inside fit/predict hot paths where a single `df.select(cols).to_numpy()` would batch the Arrow materialisation (`_dummy_baseline_regression.py:92-103`, `composite_cache.py:411`, `composite_auto_detect.py:114-209`). The pandas-Series `.values` accesses in `metrics/_ice_metric.py`, `calibration/quality.py`, `inference/predict.py`, `votenrank/*` are mostly small-scope but should migrate to `.to_numpy()` for nullable-dtype safety. Below: 30 ranked findings with concrete fixes, grouped by module.

## Strategy (priorities + biggest wins)

1. **Biggest single win**: align ALL bare `df.to_pandas()` calls in `training/_pipeline_extensions.py:497`, `training/extractors.py:249,371`, `training/core/_main_train_suite.py:814-816`, `_pipeline_helpers.py:401-402` to the project's existing `get_pandas_view_of_polars_df` Arrow split-blocks bridge. Bridge is already in `training/utils.py` and used in 4+ places; the offenders are just forgotten downgrades. Expected: ~30x on multi-million-row frames per the project's own benchmark commentary.
2. **Second-biggest**: `pl.from_pandas(...)` calls in `_phase_helpers_fit_pipeline.py:521`, `_predict_main_from_models.py:246` lack `rechunk=False` -- on the back-merge path (every predict call), rechunking a 100GB frame is the entire latency. Single-kwarg fix.
3. **Per-column .to_numpy() loops** (`composite_cache.py:411`, `composite_auto_detect.py:114-349`, `_dummy_baseline_regression.py:92-103`, `baseline_diagnostics.py:722,767`): consolidate into one `df.select(cols).to_numpy()`. The pattern in `_phase_helpers_fit_split.py:430-432` already proves the win (~Nx where N=column count).
4. **dtype-gate ALL `to_numpy(...).astype(dtype, copy=False)` chains** in `feature_engineering/transformer/*`: when `arr.dtype == dtype`, the astype is a no-op call BUT polars's `.to_numpy()` already may have copied. Add `allow_copy=False` polars hint where the frame is uniformly numeric.
5. **Modules to move polars-first**: `metrics/_ice_metric.py`, `metrics/_fairness_metrics.py`, `inference/predict.py`, `votenrank/*` still default to pandas `.values`. Migrate to `.to_numpy()` for nullable-dtype safety. Cosmetic but blocks future Int8/Float32 nullable adoption.
6. **Skip** the calibration, evaluation, votenrank conversions that are all genuinely on small (<1MB) artifacts.

## Не нашёл

- No `np.asarray(<DataFrame>)` calls (the few `np.asarray(...)` instances all wrap already-coerced ndarrays or 1-D Series; not a frame densification path).
- No `pl.from_numpy()` outside `_benchmarks/`. Bench-only call at `_benchmarks/bench_arch_d.py:232` is harmless.
- No `np.array(<DataFrame>)` calls (zero hits in target dirs).
- `inference/` dir has zero polars conversions -- inference path is already pandas-/ndarray-native upstream of the predict boundary.
- No GPU-side cupy/torch conversions in the targeted dirs (would belong under `training/neural/`; the `to_numpy()` calls in `training/neural/data.py:111-196` are PyTorch-tensor prep, all NECESSARY).
